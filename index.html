🌊 About Chilika Preserve

Chilika Lake, located in Odisha, India, is Asia's largest brackish water lagoon and a designated Ramsar Wetland of International Importance. Spanning 1,100 sq.km, it serves as a critical habitat for diverse aquatic life.

Declared a bird sanctuary in 1981, Chilika hosts over 150 migratory bird species from as far as Siberia, Caspian Sea, and Mongolia during winter. The preserve plays a vital role in biodiversity conservation and sustainable ecotourism.

🐬 Our Precious Wildlife

🐬

Irrawaddy Dolphin

Endangered species with only 150 individuals. Chilika is their only habitat in India. These gentle dolphins are a major attraction.

🦅

Migratory Birds

Purple Moorhen, Siberian Crane, Flamingo, and 150+ species visit annually. Nalabana Island is a key bird sanctuary.

🐟

52 Fish Species

From Seabass to Mullets, Chilika supports rich fishery. Traditional fishing communities depend on sustainable practices.

🦀

Crustaceans & Molluscs

Tiger Prawns, Mud Crabs, and Bivalves thrive in brackish waters, supporting local economy and biodiversity.

🌱 Conservation Efforts

🐬

Dolphin Protection

24/7 monitoring, anti-poaching patrols, and research programs ensure Irrawaddy dolphin safety.

🛶

Sustainable Tourism

Regulated boat tours, capacity limits, and eco-friendly practices protect the fragile ecosystem.

🔬

Research Station

Chilika Development Authority conducts water quality monitoring, species census, and habitat restoration.

🌿

Mangrove Restoration

Planting native mangroves to combat erosion and provide fish nurseries and bird habitats.
